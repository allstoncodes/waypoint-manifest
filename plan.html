01 POIs

Auckland Sky Tower

Standing at 328 metres, the Sky Tower is New Zealand's tallest freestanding structure and Auckland's most iconic landmark. It offers three observation decks with 360° panoramic views of the city, volcanoes, and harbour. Thrill-seekers can try the SkyJump bungee or the SkyWalk, while the revolving Orbit 360° restaurant offers fine dining above the clouds.

  • Best time: Visit at golden hour (late afternoon) or after dark for illuminated city views. Morning visits avoid peak crowds.
  • Advance booking: required
  • UV note: May is autumn in Auckland — UV Index is moderate (3–4) but still requires SPF 30+ during midday (11am–2pm). The observation deck is fully exposed; wear a hat and sunglasses. Best outdoor time is before 10am or after 3pm.
  • Booking: https://www.skycityauckland.co.nz/attractions/sky-tower/

Auckland War Memorial Museum

Housed in a neoclassical building atop Observatory Hill in Auckland Domain, this world-class museum showcases Māori and Pacific Island culture, New Zealand natural history, and military heritage. Daily Māori cultural performance shows are a highlight. It is considered one of the finest museums in the Southern Hemisphere.

  • Best time: Mornings (10am–12pm) are least crowded. Cultural shows run daily — check the schedule online. Plan 2–3 hours minimum.
  • Advance booking: No
  • UV note: The museum itself is entirely indoors — zero UV exposure inside. Auckland Domain surrounding the museum is open parkland; if walking the grounds, apply SPF 30+ between 11am–2pm in May. Plenty of shade from mature trees along pathways.
  • Booking: https://www.aucklandmuseum.com/visit/plan-your-visit

Waiheke Island Day Trip (Hauraki Gulf)

A 35-minute ferry from downtown Auckland drops you on Waiheke Island, known for its award-winning vineyards, art galleries, olive groves, and beautiful beaches. The broader Hauraki Gulf encompasses 16 islands offering hiking, swimming, birdwatching, and dolphin-watching. Waiheke is the most visitor-friendly for a day trip from the city.

  • Best time: Take the first morning ferry (~8am) to maximise your day. May has fewer crowds and cooler, comfortable temperatures ideal for vineyard tours.
  • Advance booking: required
  • UV note: May in Auckland brings moderate UV (index 3–4). Vineyard terraces and coastal walks are fully exposed. Wear SPF 50+, a broad-brimmed hat, and a long-sleeved layer. Seek shade between 11am–2pm during outdoor winery lunches. Evening ferry returns are UV-safe.
  • Booking: https://www.fullers.co.nz/destinations/waiheke-island/

Sydney Opera House

UNESCO World Heritage Site and Australia's most iconic building, the Sydney Opera House was designed by Jørn Utzon and opened in 1973. Distinguished by its soaring white ceramic-tiled shell roof evoking sails, it hosts over 1,500 performances annually. Daily guided tours reveal the engineering genius behind the design.

  • Best time: Morning guided tours (from 9am) offer the best access and lighting for photography. Book a performance for evenings to experience it at night.
  • Advance booking: required
  • UV note: Sydney in May has a moderate UV Index (~3–4), with burn time around 30 minutes. The Opera House forecourt is fully exposed to Circular Quay with no shade. Apply SPF 50+, wear sunglasses, and minimize time on the outdoor promenade between 11am–1pm. Morning and late-afternoon visits are safest.
  • Booking: https://www.sydneyoperahouse.com/visit/tours

Bondi Beach & Bondi to Coogee Coastal Walk

Australia's most famous beach, Bondi Beach, features a crescent of golden sand and an energetic surf culture. The world-renowned Bondi to Coogee coastal walk (6km, ~2 hours one-way) hugs sandstone cliffs past sea pools, parks, and hidden coves, ending in the quieter Coogee Beach neighbourhood.

  • Best time: May is autumn — cooler temps make the coastal walk ideal (16–19°C). Start by 8–9am to avoid midday UV and crowds. Weekdays are far quieter than weekends.
  • Advance booking: No
  • UV note: The entire coastal walk is sun-exposed with minimal shade over cliffs and headlands. In May, Sydney UV is moderate (~3–4) — still enough to cause skin damage in 30 minutes. Wear SPF 50+, a hat, and UV-protective clothing. Start early (before 10am) or late afternoon to avoid peak UV window of 11am–1pm.
  • Booking: https://www.sydney.com/destinations/sydney/sydney-east/bondi/attractions/bondi-to-coogee-walk

Sydney Harbour Bridge Climb

One of the world's great adventure experiences, the BridgeClimb takes small guided groups to the 134-metre summit of the iconic 1932 steel arch bridge. From the top, panoramic views sweep across the Opera House, harbour, and city skyline. Sunrise and twilight climbs are especially spectacular.

  • Best time: Book sunrise or twilight climbs for dramatic lighting and cooler temperatures. May daylight hours are shorter, making twilight climbs very popular — book 4–6 weeks in advance.
  • Advance booking: required
  • UV note: The entire climb is fully exposed at height with no shade whatsoever. BridgeClimb provides a full-body suit, but bring SPF 50+ for your face and hands. In May, peak UV risk is between 10am–2pm. Twilight climbs after 4pm are the best UV-safe option for this attraction.
  • Booking: https://www.bridgeclimb.com/

Federation Square & Melbourne Laneways (Hosier Lane)

Federation Square on the Yarra River is the cultural heart of Melbourne, housing the Ian Potter Centre NGV Australia, ACMI (Australian Centre for the Moving Image), and Koorie Heritage Trust. Nearby, Hosier Lane is Melbourne's most photographed street — an open-air gallery of ever-changing world-class street art, graffiti, and stencil work.

  • Best time: Any time of day works. Street art is best photographed in morning light. Federation Square hosts free events — check the calendar at fedsquare.com.
  • Advance booking: No
  • UV note: Melbourne in May has a moderate UV Index (around 3) with burn time approximately 30 minutes — lower risk than summer but still requires sunscreen for midday exposure. Hosier Lane is a narrow shaded laneway — low UV risk. Federation Square is open plazas with some shade structures. Apply SPF 30+ for midday visits.
  • Booking: https://www.fedsquare.com/

Royal Botanic Gardens Melbourne & Shrine of Remembrance

Melbourne's Royal Botanic Gardens span 38 hectares along the Yarra River, featuring lush themed gardens including Fern Gully, the Ornamental Lake, and native plant collections. Nearby, the Shrine of Remembrance (built 1934) is Victoria's national war memorial offering panoramic city views from its upper balcony.

  • Best time: Morning visits (9–11am) offer the best light for photography and cooler autumn temperatures. The popular 'Tan' jogging track circles the gardens.
  • Advance booking: No
  • UV note: May in Melbourne brings moderate UV levels (index ~3), with burn time around 30 minutes. The Botanic Gardens have extensive tree canopy providing excellent natural shade — seek the Fern Gully and wooded paths for UV protection. Avoid the open Ornamental Lake area between 11am–1pm without sun protection. The Shrine's exterior terrace is fully exposed.
  • Booking: https://www.rbg.vic.gov.au/visit-melbourne/

Great Ocean Road Day Trip (Twelve Apostles)

One of Australia's most spectacular road trips, the Great Ocean Road runs 243km southwest of Melbourne past dramatic surf beaches, rainforest, and the iconic Twelve Apostles — towering limestone sea stacks rising from the Southern Ocean. Currently 7 stacks remain and are rarely missed by visitors to Melbourne. Note: Parts of the road are in recovery after early 2026 flooding — check road status before visiting.

  • Best time: Depart Melbourne before 7am to reach the Twelve Apostles by mid-morning before tour buses arrive. May has fewer tourists and dramatic moody skies. A full day is required (10–12 hours round trip).
  • Advance booking: required
  • UV note: Clifftop viewing platforms at the Twelve Apostles are completely exposed to Southern Ocean winds and sun with zero shade. In May, UV is moderate (~3) but ocean reflection amplifies exposure. Wear SPF 50+, a hat, and windproof jacket. Morning arrival (before 10am) minimises UV risk. Stay on boardwalks to protect against wind-burned skin.
  • Booking: https://www.visitgreatoceanroad.org.au/plan/tours/

National Gallery of Victoria (NGV)

Australia's oldest and most visited art museum, the NGV spans two magnificent buildings — NGV International on St Kilda Road and NGV Australia in Federation Square. Together they house Australian, Indigenous, European, Asian, and contemporary international collections. Entry to the permanent collection is free.

  • Best time: Open daily 10am–5pm. Visit on weekday mornings for a relaxed experience. Check ngv.vic.gov.au for major touring exhibitions requiring timed entry.
  • Advance booking: No
  • UV note: Entirely indoors — no UV exposure risk. This is a perfect refuge during Melbourne's midday UV window (11am–1pm). Combined with a shaded walk through the adjacent Kings Domain gardens, you can structure your day around indoor attractions during peak UV hours.
  • Booking: https://www.ngv.vic.gov.au/visit/
